## Why this case matters

This case makes a structural problem easy to see.

The original paper still exists as a stable publication artifact.
But the knowledge status of its core claim did not stay still.
It was amplified, challenged, weakened, and then partially re-read through a narrower descendant path.

In a static publication system, those later changes are scattered across papers, statements, corrections, and memory.
In a living knowledge system, they should remain attached to the claim lineage itself.

---

## Original claim neighborhood

The original 2010 paper reported that high-power posing produced:

- higher testosterone,
- lower cortisol,
- greater feelings of power,
- and more tolerance for risk.

In this pilot, the original claim neighborhood is represented by:

- [`C-0001`](../objects/claims/C-0001.md) — original strong-form claim
- [`E-0001`](../objects/evidence/E-0001.md) — initial publication and early amplification context

Canonical source ids used here:

- `Carney_Cuddy_Yap_2010`
- `Early_public_amplification_context`

See [`../references.md`](../references.md) for the current source map.

## What changed later

The case did not collapse in one step.
It was hit by empirical challenge, internal withdrawal of support, broader methodological attack, and then a split into a weaker descendant route.

### 1. Empirical challenge
A larger replication challenge appeared and directly targeted the core effect.

- Object: [`D-0001`](../objects/dissents/D-0001.md)
- Canonical source id: `Ranehill_et_al_2015`

### 2. Internal collapse of support
Support weakened from inside the original authorship chain.

- Object: [`D-0002`](../objects/dissents/D-0002.md)
- Canonical source id: `Dana_Carney_2016_statement`

### 3. Methodological attack
The surrounding evidentiary profile also came under methodological attack.

- Object: [`D-0003`](../objects/dissents/D-0003.md)
- Canonical source id: `Simmons_Simonsohn_2016`

### 4. Lineage split
The case did not end cleanly.
Instead, the pilot models a weaker descendant path.

- Object: [`C-0002`](../objects/claims/C-0002.md)
- Canonical source ids: `Dana_Carney_2016_statement`, `TED_Corrections_2017`

---

## Why the descendant claim is a separate object

The weaker path is not treated here as a casual rewording of the original claim.
It is treated as a different claim in the same lineage.

That distinction matters because real knowledge does not always behave like a binary switch.
A claim can weaken, split, retreat, and survive in narrower descendant form.

This is exactly the sort of history that static publication artifacts hide badly.
