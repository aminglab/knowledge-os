## Why this case matters

This case makes a structural problem easy to see.

The original paper still exists as a stable publication artifact.
But the knowledge status of its core claim did not stay still.
It was amplified, challenged, weakened, and then partially re-read through a narrower descendant path.

In a static publication system, those later changes are scattered across papers, statements, corrections, and memory.
In a living knowledge system, they should remain attached to the claim lineage itself.

---

## Original claim neighborhood

The original 2010 paper reported that high-power posing produced:

- higher testosterone,
- lower cortisol,
- greater feelings of power,
- and more tolerance for risk.

In this pilot, the original claim neighborhood is represented by:

The case did not end cleanly.

- Object: [`C-0002`](../objects/claims/C-0002.md)

---

## Why the descendant claim is a separate object

The weaker path is not treated here as a casual rewording of the original claim.
It is treated as a different claim in the same lineage.

That matters because real knowledge does not always behave like a binary switch.
A claim can weaken, split, retreat, and survive in narrower descendant form.

This is exactly the sort of history that static publication artifacts hide badly.
