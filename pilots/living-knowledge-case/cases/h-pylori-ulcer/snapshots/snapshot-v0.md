## Why this case matters

This case matters because it shows a second knowledge fate that static publication systems also handle badly.

A claim can be wrongly rejected for years, then later become so strong that it changes how a disease is diagnosed and treated.

That means a living knowledge system must be able to represent not only weakening and failure, but also delayed vindication and later stabilization.
