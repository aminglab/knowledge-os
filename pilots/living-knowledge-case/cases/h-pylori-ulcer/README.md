# H. pylori and Peptic Ulcer Disease

This is the second case opened inside the **Living Knowledge Case Pilot**.

## Why this case

This case is useful because it exposes the opposite knowledge fate from the first public case.

The `power-posing` case shows false elevation:

- a claim becomes highly visible,
- then later weakens under replication failure and dissent.

This case is different.
It shows false rejection moving toward stabilization:

- a causal claim is initially resisted,
- major dissent focuses on biological plausibility and causal interpretation,
- later evidence and treatment response change the judgment,
- and the claim eventually rewrites clinical practice.

That makes it a good demonstration of:

- claim introduction,
- evidence accumulation,
- dissent persistence,
- verdict stabilization,
- lineage narrowing,
- public-risk guardrails,
- and delayed vindication.

## Current scope

This case is not yet a full public-layer release like the first case.
It is still the first shell-reuse test under a second payload.

But it has now completed:

- first consistency pass,
- first governed public-layer pass,
- first public-layer verification / acceptance pass,
- the first richer hardening lift on the claim-page side,
- the second richer hardening lift on the source-page side,
- the third richer hardening lift on the snapshot-subsection side,
- the fourth richer hardening lift through fuller snapshot consistency checking,
- the fifth richer hardening lift through atlas-governance self-checking,
- and the sixth richer hardening lift through public-layer orchestration boundary governance.

That means the current public claim, source, and snapshot surfaces are no longer being left purely informal.
They now have:

- explicit section obligations,
- case-scoped checkers,
- a suite entrypoint,
- a verification atlas,
- an acceptance verdict at the current threshold,
- claim-page direct pressure coverage,
- source-page role anchors,
- snapshot subsection semantic anchors,
- fuller snapshot consistency checking,
- atlas-governance self-checking,
- and public-layer orchestration boundary governance.

Its current job remains narrower than the first case:

> to prove that the first living-case shell can carry a second, structurally different knowledge fate without cloning the first case’s content,
> while progressively governing the second case’s own public-facing layers.

The current stage verdict is therefore narrower than full public release and stronger than mere seeding:

> the second case now holds an accepted first governed public layer,
> with richer hardening now landed on the claim-page, source-page, snapshot-side, atlas-governance, and orchestration-boundary surfaces,
> while still retaining holds against full public release, page emission, a larger public-layer orchestration boundary, and repository-wide public-layer generalization.
