# Snapshot Section Layering v1

This note records the current **snapshot section layering rule** for the `h-pylori-ulcer` case.

It exists because `snapshot-v0.md` is no longer just a scratch note.
It now acts as the current **whole-case release-view seed** for the second case.

That means it needs structure.
Not ornamental structure, but governed section structure.

---

## One-sentence ruling

Current ruling:

> **The snapshot is the current release-view layer for the second case.**
> **Its sections must keep judgment, claim-neighborhood, change-history, reading-path, and included-object surfaces legible and distinct.**

Do not let it decay back into one undifferentiated long page.

---

## Why this note exists now

The second case already has multiple public-facing layers:

- claim pages,
- source pages,
- and the main snapshot seed.

The snapshot has a different job from the other two.
It is not supposed to be:

- a raw object file,
- a claim-specific page,
- or a source-specific page.

It is the current release-view surface over the case as a whole.

That means the snapshot must hold together several reading jobs at once:

- tell the reader what this page is,
- expose the current visible judgment,
- explain why the case matters,
- show the core claim neighborhood,
- explain what changed later,
- and provide a stable reading path into deeper layers.

Without a section grammar, this is the surface most likely to drift first because it is the most public and the most narrative.

---

## Layer split

### Layer A. governed object and metadata layers

These layers hold:

- claim objects,
- evidence objects,
- dissent objects,
- verdict objects,
- references metadata,
- and other governed substrate.

### Layer B. claim and source page layers

These layers restore object- and source-specific public readability.

### Layer C. snapshot release-view layer

This layer gives the reader the current whole-case public entry surface.

Its job is not to replace the governed substrate.
Its job is to stage it into a legible release view.

---

## Current minimum snapshot obligations

At the current stage, `snapshot-v0.md` should expose at least the following sections:

### 1. `Title`

This section gives the reader the visible public title of the current release view.

### 2. `What this page is`

Minimum expectations:

- it should state that the page is a snapshot seed or release-view surface,
- and it should distinguish itself from a full archive or full public system.

### 3. `Current visible judgment`

Minimum expectations:

- it should include the current state of the stronger claim,
- it should include the current state of the narrower descendant claim.

### 4. `Why this case matters`

Minimum expectations:

- enough prose to explain why the case is structurally important,
- and why a living knowledge system matters here.

### 5. `Core claim neighborhood`

Minimum expectations:

- visible routes to the two current public claim pages,
- enough wording to show that the case is being treated as a lineage rather than a single frozen sentence.

### 6. `What changed later`

Minimum expectations:

- the main movement from early resistance,
- toward treatment-response and consensus uptake,
- and toward the narrower modern descendant path.

### 7. `Snapshot reading path`

Minimum expectations:

- at least one route into claim pages,
- at least one route into the reference or source layer,
- at least one route into timeline or case entry.

### 8. `Included objects`

Minimum expectations:

- the reader should be able to see the current governed object-set shape of the release view.

These are not decorative sections.
They are the minimum release-view structure of the current snapshot layer.

---

## What this note does **not** require

This note does **not** require that the snapshot:

- duplicate every claim-page section,
- duplicate every source-page section,
- become a full graph explorer,
- or freeze every paragraph of prose.

The goal is not maximal rigidity.
The goal is durable public structure.

---

## Practical verdict

So the practical verdict is:

- **the snapshot should remain the structured release-view layer**
- **its judgment, claim-neighborhood, change-history, reading-path, and included-object surfaces should remain explicit**
- **the second case should stabilize this section grammar rather than let snapshot prose drift without checks**
