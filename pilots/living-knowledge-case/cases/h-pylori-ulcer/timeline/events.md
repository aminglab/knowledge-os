# Timeline Events

## Timeline

### 1983 — early gastric-epithelium observation pressure
Early work places curved bacilli on gastric epithelium in active chronic gastritis and makes the dominant disease model less secure.

### 1984 — stronger publication pressure on the old ulcer model
Marshall and Warren publish a clearer gastritis-and-peptic-ulceration paper that moves the case from anomaly toward causal pressure.

### Early resistance period — plausibility and causality objections remain strong
The claim still faces resistance on at least two fronts:

- strong-acid plausibility resistance
- bacteria-as-bystander causality resistance

### 1994 — consensus-turn pressure
The case begins to move through consensus-level uptake rather than isolated publication alone.

### Mid-1990s — treatment-response logic hardens the case
The disease model becomes harder to resist once eradication and recurrence logic become part of the story.

### 2005 — public stabilization marker
The Nobel Prize functions as a strong public-facing stabilization marker for the stronger causal claim.

### Current reading — narrowed clinical-path descendant remains important
The modern public reading is not “every stomach complaint is H. pylori.”
It is a narrower descendant path in which testing, treatment, and retesting belong inside appropriate care pathways.
