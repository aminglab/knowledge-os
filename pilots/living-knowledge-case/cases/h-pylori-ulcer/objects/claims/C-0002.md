# Claim C-0002

## Summary

This is the narrower clinical-path descendant claim in the second case.
It keeps the durable part of the H. pylori reversal while absorbing the strongest overgeneralization objection.

## Working formulation

A concise working formulation of the claim is:

> H. pylori is not a universal explanation for all gastric or upper-GI symptoms, but in appropriate patients it is a major diagnosable and treatable cause that belongs inside modern care pathways.

## Why this object matters

A living case should not end with a triumphal reversal story.
A stabilized causal claim often yields a narrower descendant path that is better suited to real-world practice.
This object represents that narrowing.

## Working assumptions

- the stronger historical claim remains important,
- but modern public reading must be more careful,
- and current care pathways need scope constraints rather than universalization.

## Current pilot reading
