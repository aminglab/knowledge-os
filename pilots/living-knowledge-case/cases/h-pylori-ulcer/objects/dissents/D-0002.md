# Dissent D-0002

## Summary

This dissent object represents the objection that observed bacteria might be passengers or correlates rather than genuine drivers of disease.

## Core details

In the current pilot reading, this objection matters because observation alone does not settle causality.
A living case needs to preserve that distinction rather than retroactively flatten it.

## Why this matters

Without this dissent, the stronger claim would look easier than it was.
The case would lose the methodological pressure that later evidence had to answer.

## Attack surface

This dissent attacks the causal reading of both the stronger historical claim and the narrower descendant path.
