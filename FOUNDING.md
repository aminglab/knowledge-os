# Founding Note

## Knowledge should not freeze at publication

One of the strangest facts about modern civilization is that we still manage important knowledge primarily through static publication artifacts.

A paper is written, reviewed, published, indexed, cited, and archived.
But once it enters circulation, its claims are rarely governed as live objects.
Replication failures, broken evidence chains, methodological attacks, scope shrinkage, unresolved dissent, and later reversals of consensus are usually scattered across later documents, comments, review threads, blogs, conference discussions, and private memory.

The document survives.
The knowledge status changes.

That gap is the starting point for Knowledge OS.

## What we are attacking

This project is not mainly an answer engine.
It is an attack on a much older default:

> **humanity still manages dynamic knowledge through static publication artifacts**.

We already know how to publish knowledge, search knowledge, and cite knowledge.
We are still poor at governing knowledge over time.

We lack a mature lifecycle system for claims.
We lack a way to attach evidence as accountable objects.
We lack a place where dissent can remain formal instead of evaporating.
We lack visible verdicts that can be upgraded, downgraded, split, stabilized, or rejected.

## What Knowledge OS is

Knowledge OS is a system for governing the lifecycle of knowledge objects.

Its minimal public language is built around four object families:

- **Claim** — a formally stated proposition under governance
- **Evidence** — a supporting or weakening artifact linked to one or more claims
- **Dissent** — a structured challenge, reservation, attack, boundary concern, or unresolved objection
- **Verdict** — the current institutional judgment on a target claim

The primary move is simple:

> Take important knowledge work out of static documents and place it into explicit, linked, governable objects.

## What it is not

Knowledge OS is not:

- a generic chat product,
- an automated paper-generation system,
- a world model,
- a simulation engine,
- a skill marketplace,
- or a public-by-default research social network.

It may use models.
It may use skills.
It may publish documents.
But its center is neither the prompt nor the paper.
Its center is the governed knowledge object.

## What changes if this works

If this project works, a claim will no longer exist only as a sentence buried in a paper.
A claim will have a lifecycle.
It will have support, attack surface, unresolved dissent, revision history, institutional judgment, and public release history.

A public artifact will no longer be the end of knowledge work.
It will be one snapshot in a longer governed lineage.

## The immediate path

Knowledge OS does not begin with a complete standard.
It begins with live pressure.

The first task is to make the language real inside actual pilots:

- one internal, long-duration research cockpit,
- one public-facing living knowledge case.

Only then should the protocol harden.

## First principle

> **Run it alive before you freeze it.**
