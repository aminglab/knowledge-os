# Knowledge OS — Public Entry

**Knowledge OS** is a protocol layer and operating environment for governing the lifecycle of knowledge objects.

It is not a chat wrapper, not an automated paper factory, and not a static archive.
It is a system for organizing public and private knowledge work around four object families:

- **Claim**
- **Evidence**
- **Dissent**
- **Verdict**

---

## Why this exists

Important knowledge still tends to circulate as static publication artifacts.
A paper or white paper can survive for years while the standing of its core claims changes through replication failure, criticism, internal withdrawal of support, scope shrinkage, or later reinterpretation.

Knowledge OS exists to replace that **publish-and-freeze** pattern with a more visible **living governance** model.

---

## What the public layer is

The public layer is the selective release surface of Knowledge OS.
It publishes governed release views rather than only static documents.

That means a reader should be able to encounter not only:

- a summary,
- or a headline claim,

but also some visible form of:

- current judgment,
- source grounding,
- claim lineage,
- and public reading paths into the governed case.
