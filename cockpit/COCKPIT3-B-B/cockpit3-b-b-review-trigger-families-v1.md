# COCKPIT3-B-B Review Trigger Families v1

## Status

Drafted for current threshold.

## Function

This file defines the minimum family floor for `review_trigger` inside COCKPIT3-B-B.
Its purpose is to replace open-ended prose with a compact set of lawful trigger families that explain **why** a governed result surface may deserve stronger review attention without implying that review has already been opened.

---

## Governing principle

A review trigger family names a **pressure class**, not an authority class.
It answers:

> what kind of review pressure is visible here?

It does **not** answer:

> what is already authorized to happen next?

---

## Minimum trigger families

The minimum lawful trigger floor should support at least these families:

### 1. `wording_handoff_pressure`
Use when the draft is still bounded, but wording quality or explanatory framing creates a plausible need for explicit human review.

### 2. `routing_handoff_pressure`
Use when the draft proposes a route ordering, sequencing choice, or continuation framing that may deserve human review before any stronger posture is discussed.

### 3. `export_surface_pressure`
Use when the draft is still non-executable, but its likely future use as a handoff, recap, or export surface creates review pressure.

### 4. `bounded_next_step_pressure`
Use when the draft stays inside bounded next-step suggestion territory, yet the suggestion itself is strong enough that explicit review pressure should be named rather than left implicit.

---

## Unlawful trigger families

The following are not lawful trigger families at this stage:

- `execution_ready`
- `approval_pending`
- `apply_now`
- `commit_candidate`
- `publish_ready`

These names collapse review pressure into authority or action.

---

## Current lawful reading

A trigger family may explain why a draft deserves stronger human attention.
It may not claim that any current surface is already lawfully classified as `operator_review_required`.
