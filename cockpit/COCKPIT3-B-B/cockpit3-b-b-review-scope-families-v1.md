# COCKPIT3-B-B Review Scope Families v1

## Status

Drafted for current threshold.

## Function

This file defines the minimum family floor for `review_scope` inside COCKPIT3-B-B.
Its purpose is to replace ambiguous prose with a compact set of lawful scope families that explain **what the human would be reviewing** if stronger review attention were ever discussed.

---

## Governing principle

A review scope family names the **bounded object of review**, not the power granted by review.
It answers:

> what is under review?

It does **not** answer:

> what action becomes lawful after review?

---

## Minimum scope families

The minimum lawful scope floor should support at least these families:

### 1. `wording_only`
Use when review is limited to wording, explanatory framing, or bounded textual clarity.

### 2. `routing_only`
Use when review is limited to route ordering, continuation sequencing, or bounded choice among next-step framings.

### 3. `export_only`
Use when review is limited to whether the draft is suitable as a handoff, recap, or export surface.

### 4. `bounded_next_step_only`
Use when review is limited to a bounded next-step suggestion and not to any executable action or governed mutation.

---

## Unlawful scope families

The following are not lawful review-scope families at this stage:

- `mutation_scope`
- `execution_scope`
- `resolution_scope`
- `publish_scope`
- `team_control_scope`

These names overclaim authority and violate the current retained holds.

---

## Current lawful reading

A scope family may say what part of a draft would be reviewed.
It may not claim that any current surface is already lawfully opened for execution, mutation, or even fully opened operator review.
